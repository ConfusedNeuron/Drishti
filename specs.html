1. Specification Overview

The system imports a Zerodha portfolio, pulls Bloomberg data via BLPAPI (or loads from pre-exported CSV cache), computes multi-method market risk, backtests the VaR model, researches commodity factor signals across the NIFTY universe, detects volatility regimes via ML, then presents findings through a dashboard and MCP-grounded AI copilot.

Import
Zerodha, CSV, or sample holdings.
Data Pull
Bloomberg BLPAPI: prices, factors, macro, indices.
Compute Risk
VaR (3 methods), ES, stress, contribution, backtest.
Research
Factor IC, Granger, walk-forward, HMM regimes.
Explain
Risk narratives and MCP tool outputs.
Present
Dashboard, research report, copilot answers.

5. Risk Engine Specification

MetricMethodOutputCaveat to display
Historical VaR Empirical lower-tail percentile of portfolio return distribution at 95% and 99% confidence. 1-day and 10-day (square-root-time scaled) VaR amount and percentage. Past returns may not capture future regime shifts.
Parametric VaR Assumes normally distributed returns. VaR = μ - z * σ where z is the confidence quantile. 1-day and 10-day VaR. Compare to historical to assess normality assumption impact. Underestimates fat-tail losses; compare to historical and Monte Carlo.
Monte Carlo VaR Simulate 10,000 portfolio return paths using estimated covariance matrix. Take empirical quantile. 1-day and 10-day VaR with simulation confidence band. Depends on covariance estimate stability; run with multiple seeds for robustness.
Expected Shortfall Average of realized losses worse than VaR threshold. Tail loss amount, percentage, and observation count. Unstable with small samples (<250 observations).
VaR Backtest — Kupiec Likelihood ratio test: observed violations vs. expected at stated confidence. H₀: violation rate = 1 - confidence. Observed violation rate, expected rate, LR statistic, p-value, pass/fail. Low power with short windows (<250 observations).
VaR Backtest — Christoffersen Test independence of violations — violations should not cluster. Extends Kupiec with conditional coverage. Independence test statistic, p-value, combined test result. Failing independence test suggests volatility clustering not captured by model.
Regime-conditioned VaR Estimate VaR separately for high-vol and low-vol regimes identified by HMM. Report current regime VaR. VaR by regime, current regime label, regime probability. Regime labels are probabilistic; history is rewritten each time HMM is refitted.
Component VaR Marginal contribution to portfolio VaR using covariance and weights. Top risk contributors by amount and percentage share. Assumes static current weights for historical simulation.
Stress testing Apply scenario shock vector to holdings/sectors/factors. Scenario loss, affected sectors, top loss contributors. Shocks are deterministic; does not account for second-order contagion effects.

6. Factor Signal Research Specification

The spillover research module is Drishti's primary differentiator. It measures how commodity and macro shocks transmit risk into Indian equity sectors — primarily via DCC-GARCH dynamic conditional correlations and Diebold–Yilmaz connectedness (volatility spillover), with IC / Granger lead-lag as secondary descriptives. This aligns the project with the VAR-MGARCH / DCC volatility-spillover methodology standard in the Indian equity–commodity literature.

Research vs risk Factor signal research is not portfolio construction. It measures signal quality and reports statistics. It does not produce buy/sell recommendations or optimal allocations.
MetricDefinitionThreshold for significance
Information Coefficient (IC) Rank correlation (Spearman) between the factor signal at time t and forward returns at time t+lag. Computed cross-sectionally across NIFTY sectors or holdings. IC > 0.05 considered economically meaningful; IC > 0.10 strong.
IC Information Ratio (ICIR) Mean IC divided by standard deviation of IC over rolling windows. Measures IC stability. ICIR > 0.5 considered consistent; > 1.0 strong.
IC t-statistic t = IC_mean / (IC_std / sqrt(n_periods)). Tests whether mean IC is significantly different from zero. |t| > 1.96 significant at 5%; |t| > 2.58 significant at 1%.
Granger causality p-value VAR-based Granger causality test: does lagged commodity factor improve prediction of sector returns beyond the sector's own lags? p < 0.05 reject null of no Granger causality. Report all p-values including non-significant.
Walk-forward Sharpe Expand-window training with out-of-sample signal application. Go long when signal is positive, flat/short otherwise. Compute annualized Sharpe on OOS returns. Sharpe > 0.5 OOS considered promising for prototype. Primary evidence: OOS, not IS.

7. ML Model Specification

Model 1 — HMM Volatility Regime Detection

  • Type: Gaussian Hidden Markov Model, 2 hidden states (low-vol, high-vol).
  • Features: Rolling 20-day portfolio return std, rolling 20-day absolute return mean, India VIX level, VIX change.
  • Library: hmmlearn.GaussianHMM
  • Validation: Walk-forward refitting; compare regime-conditioned VaR violation rate to unconditional in OOS window.
  • Output: Current state probability, historical regime sequence, regime-conditioned VaR.

Model 2 — VaR Breach Classifier (Optional)

  • Type: Gradient Boosted Tree (XGBoost) binary classifier.
  • Target: Will tomorrow's portfolio return exceed the 99% historical VaR threshold? (breach = 1).
  • Features: Rolling volatility, VIX level, Brent 5-day return, USD/INR 5-day change, 10Y yield change, sector momentum z-scores.
  • Validation: Time-series split, no lookahead. Report AUC, precision at high-confidence threshold, and calibration.
  • Caveat: Class imbalance at 1% tail — use SMOTE or adjusted threshold carefully.
ML hygiene rules
  • All feature construction uses only information available at time t (no future data leaks).
  • Train/test split is always time-ordered — never random shuffle on time series data.
  • Report out-of-sample performance separately from in-sample. If OOS degrades significantly, say so.
  • Regime labels from HMM are refit on expanding window — do not use full-sample labels for any in-sample training.

8. Backtest Framework Specification

VaR model backtest
  1. Compute historical VaR at 99% confidence on a 252-day rolling window.
  2. For each day in the backtest window, record whether actual portfolio return exceeded the VaR estimate (violation = 1).
  3. Run Kupiec LR test: LR = -2 * log((p^x * (1-p)^(T-x)) / (x/T)^x * (1-x/T)^(T-x)) where p = 0.01, x = violations, T = days.
  4. Run Christoffersen test: independence of violations using first-order Markov transitions.
  5. Report: observed violation rate, expected 1%, p-values for both tests, pass/fail.
Factor signal walk-forward backtest
  1. Training window: minimum 252 days expanding from start date.
  2. At each refit date (monthly), compute IC on training window, identify best factor-lag pair.
  3. Apply signal to next OOS month: go long sector if signal is positive.
  4. Accumulate OOS returns across all refit periods.
  5. Report: OOS Sharpe, OOS IC, IC stability (ICIR), turnover, regime breakdown of returns.
Lookahead bias checklist
  • Prices at time t are only used to compute returns known at end of day t.
  • VaR estimate at time t uses only data through t-1.
  • Signal at time t uses factor data through t-1, not t.
  • HMM regime at time t is determined using data through t-1 in walk-forward mode.
  • Instrument master and sector map are treated as static to avoid survivorship bias for prototype scope.

9. Commodity Spillover Specification

The commodity module is both a risk-driver layer (portfolio sensitivity) and a research layer (signal quality validation). It answers two distinct questions: "How sensitive is my portfolio to commodity shocks today?" and "Do commodity factor returns have predictive power over sector returns?"

commodity_sensitivity_score =
  sector_weight
  * normalized_abs_lag_beta

  * confidence_multiplier  # = 1 if Granger p < 0.05 else 0.5

14. Acceptance Test Scenarios

  • Bloomberg cache loads prices for NIFTY 50 stocks, 8 commodity/macro series, and 7 sector indices without external network.
  • Historical, parametric, and Monte Carlo VaR differ by less than 20% on a diversified portfolio — large divergence triggers a model comparison note.
  • Kupiec test p-value > 0.05 on historical VaR at 99% for a sample portfolio with 3 years of data.
  • HMM identifies high-vol regime during COVID (Feb–Apr 2020) and rate-hike cycle (2022).
  • Brent crude factor shows IC t-stat > 1.96 against NSEOILGS Index at 1–3 day lag.
  • Walk-forward factor backtest reports OOS Sharpe separately from in-sample.
  • Copilot answers "What is my biggest risk?" citing top component VaR and current regime.
  • Copilot refuses "Should I buy more RELIANCE?" and redirects to risk diagnostics.
  • RSS failure shows stale/unavailable state without affecting core risk calculations.

Drishti — Product Requirements Document

1. Product Vision

Drishti turns a Zerodha equity portfolio into a rigorous risk and factor research workspace. It computes deterministic risk metrics, researches commodity spillover signals with quantitative validation, detects volatility regimes using ML, and surfaces all findings through a natural-language copilot — while keeping calculations transparent and auditable.

Positioning Drishti is a risk analytics and signal research tool, not a trading recommendation engine. It reports exposure, loss estimates, stress sensitivity, factor signal quality, and ML-detected regimes. All language is educational and diagnostic.
Core promise Show total market risk first, explain the drivers behind it second, research predictive factor signals third.
Project novelty Bloomberg BLPAPI pipeline + commodity→sector volatility-spillover research (DCC-GARCH, Diebold–Yilmaz) + ML volatility regime detection + FinBERT news-risk NLP + MCP-grounded AI copilot — all in one local prototype.
Resume differentiator Walk-forward backtesting, IC/t-stat signal validation, regime-conditioned VaR, and grounded LLM explanation via MCP — demonstrating quant research depth alongside engineering.

2. Problem Statement

Retail investors know their holdings and P&L but not their market risk, factor exposures, or how commodity shocks flow through their portfolio. Standard broker views never answer: "How much can this portfolio lose?", "Which commodity is driving my sector risk?", or "Is my VaR model working?"

Current gapDrishti responseFRM / quant concept demonstrated
Holdings are shown as a list, not a risk system. Compute VaR (3 methods), ES, stress losses, drawdown, concentration, component contribution. Market risk measurement, portfolio aggregation, model comparison.
Commodity shocks affect Indian sectors with lagged effects not visible in holdings screens. Research commodity factor signals — IC, Granger causality, walk-forward Sharpe — across NIFTY universe. Factor models, signal quality, lead-lag analysis, alpha research methodology.
VaR models assume stable volatility but markets have regimes. Detect high/low volatility regimes via HMM, produce regime-conditioned VaR estimates. Volatility clustering, regime switching, model risk, GARCH.
VaR is computed but never validated. Run Kupiec and Christoffersen backtests on VaR model; report violation rates and p-values. Backtesting, statistical testing, model validation.
News is noisy and hard to connect to portfolio risk. Tag RSS headlines by sector/ticker and surface event risk context. Qualitative risk overlay and event risk.

3. Target Users

Primary demo user Professor or student with a Zerodha account who wants a real portfolio risk and factor research diagnosis during the presentation.
Quant interviewer Hiring manager at a hedge fund, bank quant desk, or DS team who wants to see signal research methodology, backtesting rigor, and ML application.
Production user Retail investor who wants educational risk analytics and commodity exposure context without trade recommendations.

4. MVP Scope

In scope for prototype
  • Local web dashboard with Python backend.
  • Bloomberg BLPAPI data pipeline with CSV-export fallback. New
  • Zerodha import where credentials are available; CSV/sample fallback.
  • Historical VaR (3 methods), ES, stress tests, concentration, drawdown.
  • VaR backtesting: Kupiec test, Christoffersen test, violation rate. New
  • Commodity→sector risk-transmission research: DCC-GARCH dynamic correlations + Diebold–Yilmaz spillover (IC/Granger as secondary lead-lag). New
  • ML volatility regime detection (HMM 2-state) and regime-conditioned VaR. New
  • Cross-sectional research across NIFTY 200; dashboard on NIFTY 50 + sector indices (Energy, Metals, FMCG, IT). New
  • Macro factors: USD/INR, India 10Y G-Sec yield, India VIX. New
  • News-risk NLP: FinBERT sentiment (VADER fallback) over NSE-Cogencis / SEBI / NSE headlines, tagged by sector & ticker.
  • Dual MCP demo: Zerodha Kite MCP plus custom Risk MCP.
  • AI copilot grounded exclusively on structured risk and factor outputs.
Out of scope for prototype
  • Order placement or portfolio rebalancing instructions.
  • Buy/sell/hold recommendations of any kind.
  • Full F&O Greeks and margin risk.
  • Mutual fund look-through decomposition.
  • Paid news subscriptions or full article scraping.
  • Multi-user production account management.
  • Public redistribution of Bloomberg or exchange market data.
  • Live WebSocket streaming (prototype uses daily EOD data).

  2. Presenter imports portfolio — live Zerodha, CSV upload, or pre-loaded sample.
  3. Risk engine runs: VaR, ES, stress scenarios, Kupiec backtest, HMM regime.

  5. Presenter asks copilot: What is the biggest risk and is the VaR model reliable?

8. Product Risks And Mitigations

RiskImpactMitigation
BLPAPI access not available from laptop on FRTL network Programmatic data pull blocked Pre-export Bloomberg data to CSV from terminal; build cache loader that accepts both BLPAPI and CSV input. Cache before demo day.
Zerodha access unavailable during presentation Live demo could stall Keep sample and CSV import as first-class paths. Pre-load Bloomberg cache for sample portfolio.
Factor signals are statistically weak for some periods Spillover module may overclaim Report IC, t-stat, and "not significant" states explicitly. Weak result is still a valid research finding.
HMM regime model overfits in-sample Regime-conditioned VaR looks better than it is Use walk-forward regime detection. Report in-sample vs. out-of-sample VaR violation rates separately.
LLM hallucinates risk conclusions Incorrect explanation or advice-like wording Feed only structured risk engine outputs to LLM. Enforce "not advice" system policy.
Bloomberg data redistribution concern Terms of service violation if data is shared publicly Never commit raw Bloomberg data to a public repository. Keep all price data in local cache only. Use only for academic analysis.

9. Prototype Vs Production

Prototype
  • Runs locally on presenter machine.
  • Bloomberg data pre-cached via BLPAPI or manual CSV export.
  • One account/session at a time.
  • Local SQLite or file cache.
  • No persistent token storage.
  • Manual .env secrets.
  • Sample data guarantees demo continuity.
Production
  • Hosted frontend and backend.
  • Licensed market data subscription (not Bloomberg terminal — e.g., Kite Connect paid plan, NSE data vendor).
  • Encrypted token storage with short retention.
  • Consent screen, privacy policy, and user account management.
  • Rate-limit-aware queues and background data refresh jobs.
  • Audit logs and policy checks for AI output.
  • SEBI/legal review before personalized retail launch.

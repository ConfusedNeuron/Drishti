1. Product Vision

Portfolio Risk Copilot helps a Zerodha user understand where portfolio market risk is coming from. The product turns a holdings list into a risk dashboard and a natural-language explanation layer, while keeping calculations deterministic and auditable.

Positioning The product must never be framed as a buy/sell recommendation engine. It reports exposure, loss estimates, stress sensitivity, concentration, commodity spillover, and news-linked risk flags. The final user-facing language is educational and diagnostic.
Core promise Show total market risk first, then explain the drivers behind that risk.
Project novelty Combine portfolio VaR/ES with commodity spillover, RSS news context, and MCP-based AI interaction.
Demo strategy Run locally, connect a live Zerodha account if available, and fall back to sample/CSV data instantly.

2. Problem Statement

Retail investors often know their holdings and unrealized P&L but do not know their market risk. Standard broker views usually emphasize prices, positions, and orders; they rarely answer questions such as: "How much can this portfolio lose in a bad market?", "Which stock drives the risk?", or "Is this portfolio exposed to crude oil shocks?"

Current gap Risk copilot response FRM concept demonstrated
Holdings are shown as a list, not a risk system. Convert holdings into weights, returns, volatility, VaR, ES, and stress losses. Market risk measurement and portfolio aggregation.
Investors may mistake diversification by number of stocks for true diversification. Compute concentration, sector exposure, correlation, and component VaR. Diversification, correlation risk, and marginal contribution.
Commodity shocks affect Indian sectors with lagged effects but are not visible in holdings screens. Model crude/gold/copper/gas spillover into sector and portfolio risk. Factor risk, lead-lag analysis, spillover, stress testing.
News is noisy and hard to connect to portfolio risk. Tag RSS headlines by sector/ticker and summarize risk context. Qualitative risk overlay and event risk.

3. Target Users

Primary demo user Professor or student with a Zerodha account who wants a real portfolio risk diagnosis during the presentation.
Fallback user Class audience viewing a realistic sample portfolio when live auth is unavailable.
Production user Retail investor who wants educational risk analytics without receiving trade recommendations.

4. MVP Scope

In scope for prototype
  • Local web dashboard with Python backend.
  • Zerodha import where credentials/access are available.
  • CSV/manual/sample portfolio fallback.
  • Deep modeling for equity and ETF holdings.
  • Historical VaR, Expected Shortfall, stress tests, concentration, sector risk.
  • Commodity spillover diagnostics using public or cached factor data.
  • RSS headline risk context from free feeds.
  • Dual MCP demo: Zerodha Kite MCP plus custom Risk MCP.
Out of scope for prototype
  • Order placement or portfolio rebalancing instructions.
  • Buy/sell/hold recommendations.
  • Full F&O Greeks and margin risk.
  • Mutual fund look-through risk decomposition.
  • Paid news subscriptions or full article scraping.
  • Multi-user production account management.
  • Public redistribution of exchange market data.

6. Demo User Journey

1. Open app
Presenter starts the local dashboard.
2. Import
Connect Zerodha or load sample/CSV portfolio.
3. Analyze
Backend computes market risk and factor diagnostics.
4. Explain
Dashboard shows risk drivers and news context.
5. Ask AI
Copilot answers using MCP tools and structured context.
  1. The presenter asks a professor/student for voluntary Zerodha access.
  2. If no one volunteers, the presenter loads a prepared sample portfolio.
  3. The app maps holdings to sectors and obtains cached/public price history.
  4. The risk engine produces the dashboard and downloadable memo.
  5. The presenter asks the copilot: What is the biggest risk in this portfolio?

8. Product Risks And Mitigations

Risk Impact Mitigation
Zerodha access unavailable during presentation Live demo could stall Keep sample and CSV import as first-class paths.
Personal/free API does not provide all desired market data Historical calculations may fail if dependent on paid endpoints Use public/cached historical prices; document paid Kite Connect as production enhancement.
LLM hallucinates risk conclusions Incorrect explanation or advice-like wording Give only structured risk engine outputs to the LLM and enforce "not advice" response policy.
Commodity relation is statistically weak for some portfolios Spillover module may overclaim Report confidence, lag window, and "not significant" states explicitly.
Regulatory/compliance concerns Production launch could require SEBI/legal review Position as education and analytics; avoid personalized trade recommendations.

9. Prototype Vs Production

Prototype
  • Runs locally on presenter machine.
  • One account/session at a time.
  • Local SQLite or file cache.
  • No persistent token storage.
  • Manual .env secrets.
  • Public/cached data for price history.
  • Sample data guarantees demo continuity.
Production
  • Hosted frontend and backend.
  • Encrypted token storage with short retention.
  • Consent screen, privacy policy, and user account management.
  • Rate-limit-aware queues and background jobs.
  • Paid Kite Connect plan if live/historical data is needed at scale.
  • No public redistribution of exchange market data.
  • Audit logs and policy checks for AI output.
